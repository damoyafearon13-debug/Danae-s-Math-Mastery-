üîë Steps:

‚ú® Topic 3: Simplifying Expressions

Simplifying means making expressions shorter by combining like terms and expanding brackets.

üîë Like Terms: Terms with same variable part
‚Ä¢ 5x and 3x ARE like terms (both have x)
‚Ä¢ 4y¬≤ and 2y¬≤ ARE like terms (both have y¬≤)
‚Ä¢ 5x and 3y are NOT like terms (different variables)

Collecting Like Terms:

Only add/subtract terms with same variable: 5x + 3x = 8x ‚úì

Expanding Brackets:

Multiply everything inside by what's outside: 3(x + 4) = 3x + 12

Example: Simplify 4a + 7 - 2a + 3

Group like terms: (4a - 2a) + (7 + 3)
‚Üí 2a + 10

Example: Simplify 3(x + 4)

Multiply 3 by each term inside
‚Üí 3x + 12

Example: Simplify 4(a + 2) + 3a

Expand: 4a + 8 + 3a
Collect: 7a + 8
